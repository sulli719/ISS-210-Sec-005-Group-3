About

Socioeconomic status greatly impacts both quality of life in the current economy and the ability to contribute in scoiety. For those that are able-bodied According to the U.S. Department of Labor, only 17.9% of persons with disabilities were employed in 2020 compared to the employment rate of 61.8% for people without disabilities. Although this data may be a reflection of how the COVID-19 pandemic has impacted employment rates, the numbers are still troubling. Thus, our group has decided to investigate the issue of how socioeconomic status impacts job opportunities for workers . More specifically, we have decided to focus on the environments of which persons with disabilities work in and how they are treated.
Following the signing of the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) of 1990 discrimination remains a pressing issue in the workplace, President Bush hoped to bring equality in employment opportunities for individuals with disabilities. Years later, disability discrimination remains a pressing issue within the workplace.

Why is it so hard to escape poverty? - Ann-Helén Bay

The video, “Why is it so hard to escape poverty? - Ann-Helén Bay” talks about the welfare trap, which is one of the poverty traps affecting millions of people and not allowing them to move up to a higher socioeconomic status, or simply escaping poverty. The benefit programs provided by the government seem good, which can help people who are in need can easily save money and escape poverty, but that’s not true, because only people who fall below a certain income level are eligible for benefits, meaning if some people make just enough to be disqualified from the program, only income they would get is from their work, but if those jobs don’t give them enough money as what they have been getting being unemployed, they might quit the job and just relies on the benefit program. This video is related to the topic, “how socioeconomic status impacts job opportunities for workers” because people with low socioeconomic status are most likely to have a lack of education which leads to not many job opportunities, especially with enough income that covers their basic needs and the extra money they can save for the future.
